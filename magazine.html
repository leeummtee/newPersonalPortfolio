Magazine Spreads

making print spreads for a Toronto Raptors themed magazine.

01. summary

objective

Magazine spreads for a Graphic Design class. Students were given a two weeks to create print spreads for a topic of their choosing using all of the provided "dummy" text.

programs used

Adobe InDesign and Photoshop

02. process

a. sketches

The process began with me coming up with some sketches for the general layout of the spreads.

b. grid system

Using my sketches as reference, I started creating my first mock-up in Adobe Illustrator. Seeing things in a higher fidelity made me realize that Discord's name needed to stand out more, so I also ended up placing its name on a shooting star to symbolize how it can be the answer to students’ wishes for an easier way to communicate on projects online.

c. outcome

My initial feedback from my peers was that a banner-style poster with so much information would be overwhelming for students to look at. Based on their critiques, I decided to shrink it down to an A0 size by cutting out the steps and replacing it with a summary of Discord’s features.

03. reflection

Working on this project made me realize how important it is for a poster to able to get a lot of information across with just a quick glance. My first design had too much content and would require a passerby to thoroughly read through all of it to get the full meaning behind the poster. However, I feel that the changes I made allowed for a much clearer message to be communicated in a much quicker way.
